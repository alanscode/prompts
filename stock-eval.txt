Structure and Instructions:
1. Executive Summary
3–5 concise bullet points distilling the investment case, valuation outlook, key support zones, and near-term catalysts/risks.

2. Business Overview & Strategy
Describe the company’s core products, services, and revenue segments.

Identify main business lines, geographic/sectoral exposure, and major customer types.

Summarize competitive positioning, key partners/suppliers, and pertinent macroeconomic or regulatory factors.

3. Financial Analysis
Present data from the last 3 full fiscal years PLUS the latest TTM period.

Include: revenue growth, gross/operating/net margins, free cash flow, capex, cash/debt/liquidity.

Show key ratios (ROE, ROIC, P/E, EV/EBITDA) in a clear table of historical figures.

4. Recent News & Events
Summarize major events from the prior 12 months (earnings, guidance, management changes, launches, M&A, litigation, regulation), referencing dates and sources.

5. Industry & Market Trends
Analyze sector/industry landscape and macro drivers.

Include: TAM, competitive dynamics, demand trends, and important emerging technologies.

6. Quantitative Support Zone Analysis (Multi-Factor Confluence)
a. Technical Confluence:

Identify price levels where at least 3 of the following overlap:

Volume Profile (VPVR/POC, high-volume nodes)

Fibonacci Retracements (focus on Golden Pocket: 0.618–0.65)

Dynamic Support (50 & 200-day SMA, weekly/monthly VWAP anchors)

RSI Bullish Divergence (4h/D)

b. Institutional Footprint:

Highlight recent dark pool/block trade activity or volume spikes with no price change (signs of absorption or accumulation).

Note options "Put Wall" (max put OI; gamma exposure), indicating potential market-maker defense levels.

c. Sentiment & Macro Filters:

Assess sector rotation status (accumulation/distribution vs. SPX).

Account for upcoming events (earnings, Fed dates, ex-div).

d. Output — Key Levels:

"Iron Floor" Zone: Strongest institutionally-weighted support.

"Aggressive Entry" Zone: Higher-risk support for early accumulation.

"Invalidation" Level: If breached, the bull thesis is void.

Confidence Score (1–10) based on factor alignment.

7. Bull Catalysts
List 3–5 positive drivers (short, medium, long term), highlighting moats/advantages.

8. Bear Catalysts & Downside Risks
List 3–5 key risks/headwinds (short, medium, long term), with context and likely impact.

9. Valuation Analysis
DCF with explicit, justified assumptions (growth, margins, WACC).

Comps table vs. peers and industry multiples.

(Optional) Sum-of-Parts if relevant.

Include a clear table with valuation sensitivities to key assumptions.

10. Investment Thesis
State a clear Buy/Hold/Sell recommendation, supported by quantitative and qualitative evidence.

Argue whether the stock is under-, fairly, or over-valued.

11. Price Projections & Entry Strategy
Provide targets for short term (1–3m), medium (6–18m), long term (3–5y).

For bullish cases, suggest optimal entry points, stop-loss levels, and reevaluation triggers based on technical and fundamental alignment.

12. Confidence & Key Assumptions
List primary assumptions driving both thesis and confluence levels.

Give an overall confidence rating (Low/Medium/High).

13. Appendix
Add source/valuation tables (with date-stamped citations/URLs), and any relevant charts, tables, or metric summaries.

Formatting & Output:
Use Markdown headings and bullet lists for readability.

Include at least one table summarizing financials and one for technical/valuation sensitivities.

Cite all facts, figures, and news with publication/source dates for professional traceability.

Tone: Analytical, data-driven, and objective — suitable for institutional investors and financial professionals.
